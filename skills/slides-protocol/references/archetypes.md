# v1 Slide Archetypes

Every v1 style pack should implement the same fixed set of page forms.

These archetypes are content-agnostic structures. The canonical specimen deck provides the shared sample content used to exercise them.

## Archetype list

### `title`

Purpose:
- open the deck
- establish topic, author, and tone

Typical content:
- title
- subtitle
- presenter or organization metadata

### `section`

Purpose:
- separate major parts of the narrative
- reset visual rhythm

Typical content:
- section title
- short framing line

### `thesis`

Purpose:
- state the main argument or claim

Typical content:
- one strong claim
- supporting bullets or proof points

### `concept`

Purpose:
- explain one idea, model, or definition

Typical content:
- concept title
- explanation paragraph
- structured sub-points

### `comparison`

Purpose:
- contrast two approaches, states, or systems

Typical content:
- left side
- right side
- summary takeaway

### `list`

Purpose:
- present ordered or unordered points

Typical content:
- heading
- 3 to 6 bullets

### `timeline`

Purpose:
- show change over time or staged delivery

Typical content:
- phase labels
- milestone descriptions

### `data`

Purpose:
- communicate metrics or evidence

Typical content:
- headline metric
- supporting metrics
- note or interpretation

### `media`

Purpose:
- use an image or visual as the primary content

Typical content:
- heading
- image
- caption or annotation

### `closing`

Purpose:
- close with a takeaway, request, or next step

Typical content:
- closing statement
- action or invitation

## Archetype guidance for style packs

- A style pack should expose one slice per archetype in v1.
- Slices should be designed against the canonical specimen deck, not against ad hoc user content.
- Slices may add decorative structure, but should keep editable content clearly separable from decorative layers.
- Style packs may add private helper classes and CSS, but should not invent new protocol attributes when standard HTML or existing optional hints are enough.
